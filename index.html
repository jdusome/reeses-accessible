Reese's Peanut Butter Cups

Interesting Facts

The Reese’s candy was introduced in the year 1928.

The man who created the Reese’s Peanut Butter Cup was a farmer, by the name of Harry Burnett Reese.

Reese was a former shipping foreman and dairy farmer for Milton S. Hershey, the founder of Hershey’s chocolate. Reese started working for Hershey’s in 1917, however, several years later, Reese left his job to engage in his own candy business.

The original name for Reese’s candy was “penny cups” due to their price at the time being only one cent in the early 1930’s.

There have been over 20 variations of the Reese’s Peanut Butter Cup, that had been sold as limited edition cups for various occasions, such as; Christmas, Halloween & Valentine’s Day.

The candy has its own special holiday, ‘I Love Reese’s Day’, and is celebrated across the U.S. every year on May 18. Approximately 40,000 fans joined an online campaign in 2011 to make ‘I Love Reese’s Day’ an official holiday for the candy.

Reese’s enterprise started humbly in his basement.
